About Vision 30

A transformative educational initiative building a sustainable pipeline of blind and low-vision STEM leaders across India

The Challenge We Address

Breaking barriers that prevent blind and low-vision students from accessing STEM education

Highly Visual STEM Pedagogy

Traditional teaching methods in science and mathematics rely heavily on visual representations, making it challenging for blind and low-vision students to access complex concepts.

Limited Specialized Resources

Inadequate access to Braille materials, assistive technologies, and specialized learning tools that are essential for STEM education accessibility.

Inadequate Teacher Training

Most educators lack training in inclusive teaching methods and assistive technologies needed to effectively teach STEM subjects to visually impaired students.

Digital Inaccessibility

Over 96% of digital content is inaccessible to screen readers, creating significant barriers to online learning and digital resource access.

Economic Barriers

High costs of specialized equipment, accessible materials, and quality education create financial obstacles for many families.

Lack of Role Models

Absence of visible successful professionals with visual impairments in STEM fields limits inspiration and career guidance for students.

Program Tracks

Three specialized tracks designed to meet students at different educational stages

V30-GEN

General Skill Building

Foundation track for students in Classes 8, 9, and 10 focusing on overall skill development and building strong fundamentals across all subjects.

  • Classes 8-10 students
  • Comprehensive skill building
  • Foundation in all subjects
  • Essential life skills training
Budget: ₹12.5 Lacs per year

V30-STEM

STEM Development

Specialized track for Classes 8-10 students with focused development in Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics subjects.

  • Classes 8-10 (STEM focus)
  • Advanced STEM curriculum
  • Specialized labs and equipment
  • Research methodology training
Budget (Year 1): ₹34 Lacs (6 students)

V30-HED

Higher Education

Advanced track for Classes 11-12 science stream students preparing for competitive exams and higher education opportunities.

  • Classes 11-12 (Science stream)
  • Competitive exam preparation
  • University application support
  • Advanced research projects
Budget: ₹85,000 per student/year

Selection Process

A comprehensive evaluation process to identify the most promising candidates

1

School Nomination

Students are nominated from ~10-15 select special schools across India based on their academic performance and potential.

2

Academic Assessment

Evaluation based on above-average performance in Science and Mathematics, along with teacher and principal feedback.

3

Baseline Testing

Comprehensive assessment covering Aptitude, English, Mathematics, Science, and Computer Science capabilities.

4

Motivation Assessment

Evaluation of self-drive, commitment, and willingness to dedicate extra time to preparation and skill development.

Our Mission

To create a sustainable pipeline of 30 exceptional blind and low-vision students who will excel in STEM fields, breaking barriers and creating pathways for future generations. We provide comprehensive support through academic excellence, career development, and life skills training.

Our Vision

A world where visual impairment is never a barrier to achieving excellence in science, technology, engineering, and mathematics. We envision our graduates becoming leaders, innovators, and role models who inspire others and drive inclusive development in STEM fields.

The Vision 30 Program

A comprehensive 3-year initiative designed to transform the educational journey of blind and low-vision students

Program Overview

Vision 30 is an initiative designed by VE in partnership with I-Stem to provide a holistic and inclusive educational environment for 30 promising blind and low vision students from across India, catering to their overall skill building and development: with the long-term objective of raising awareness and changing perceptions regarding the capabilities of VI students and establishing role models who can inspire and advocate for others with similar challenges. The V30 program aims to: • Identify, inspire and support deserving students with visual impairment in high schools across India to emerge as role models in the country. • Enable and empower students to achieve academic excellence and gain admission to top colleges and universities. • Develop increased confidence, independence, and social integration of students with visual impairment with others. 2024 December, Vision30’s first offline residential learning camp was launched in RKM Blind Boys’ Academy, Narendrapur, Kolkata, attended by 18 students from 7 schools for the visually impaired from 5 states across India. The 3 days camp covered the following agenda. The learning from this pilot roll out has been very positive and insightful. Based on the feedback and support received we are encouraged to scale the program in 2025 -26 to increase our reach and strengthen the program design for impact keeping a 3 year horizon in focus.

Program Components

Comprehensive support across multiple dimensions of student development

Academic Excellence

Advanced curriculum in mathematics, physics, chemistry, and computer science with specialized teaching methods adapted for blind and low-vision students.

Technology Training

Comprehensive training in assistive technologies, programming languages, and digital tools essential for STEM careers.
